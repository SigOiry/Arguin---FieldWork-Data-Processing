Atmospheric Correction

Field ASD spectra were collected across the Banc d’Arguin intertidal area to sample the dominant benthic targets, including bare sediments, seagrasses, algae, and biogenic reef structures (Figure 1).

The atmospheric-correction assessment focuses on the AC stations measured on 2026-03-27, coincident with a Sentinel-2 acquisition. Hyperspectral ASD measurements were resampled with Sentinel-2 spectral response functions to provide band-compatible in situ reflectance for comparison with satellite products (Figure 2).

Satellite reflectance was extracted at ASD station coordinates from two atmospheric correction products: the standard Sentinel-2 Level-2A Sen2Cor product and ACOLITE-derived surface reflectance (rhos). Matchups were assessed using linear regression alongside error metrics comparing image-derived reflectance with ASD measurements resampled to Sentinel-2 spectral bands.

Figure 3 presents the matchup results for both atmospheric correction approaches—Sen2Cor (left) and ACOLITE (right)—using imagery acquired on the same day as the field measurements (27 March 2026). Both methods exhibit strong agreement with ASD data, with high coefficients of determination (R² = 0.935 for Sen2Cor and 0.92 for ACOLITE).

For low reflectance values (R < 0.3), Sen2Cor-derived reflectance is relatively close to ASD measurements; however, the divergence increases at higher reflectance levels. In contrast, ACOLITE shows consistent agreement with ASD across the full reflectance range.

Although Sen2Cor yields a slightly higher R², it departs more from the 1:1 relationship. Conversely, ACOLITE remains more closely aligned with the 1:1 line, a pattern also supported by the other error metrics, indicating a better overall agreement with field measurements.

Figure 4 presents the comparison between ASD field measurements acquired on 27 March and satellite-derived reflectance from an image acquired 20 days earlier (7 March). While the temporal mismatch likely contributes to the observed differences, the comparison between atmospheric correction methods remains informative.

Sen2Cor exhibits lower dispersion relative to ASD measurements, with a higher coefficient of determination (R² = 0.845) compared to ACOLITE (R² = 0.795). However, ACOLITE shows a closer alignment with the 1:1 relationship and consistently lower error metrics (RMSD, bias, MAD, RPD, and APD).

Overall, despite the lower R², ACOLITE provides more accurate and consistent reflectance estimates, making it a more suitable choice for long-term time series analyses of the Banc d’Arguin based on satellite-derived data.
